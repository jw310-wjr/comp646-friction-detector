%%%%%%%%% ABSTRACT
\begin{abstract}
Effective teaching depends on noticing student confusion and adjusting
instruction.  We present a system that detects \emph{pedagogical friction}:
moments where a low-quality instructional strategy coincides with visible
student confusion.  Our pipeline constructs a confusion timeline from
per-frame facial affect (DeepFace~\cite{deepface}), a strategy timeline
from transcribed speech (Whisper~\cite{whisper}) annotated by heuristics,
temporally aligns both signals on a 30-second grid, and flags candidate
windows via a statistical $z$-score filter.  Flagged windows are passed to
Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} for friction confirmation and
alternative-strategy generation.  CPU smoke tests on the TIMSS AU1
\emph{Exterior Angles} clip (5\,min) yield \textbf{3 friction candidates};
VLM fusion and human-label evaluation are ongoing.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

High-quality teaching requires moment-by-moment diagnosis of student
understanding and rapid strategy adjustment~\cite{sherinvanes2009}.
Tools such as Tutor CoPilot~\cite{tutorcopilot} and
Edu-ConvoKit~\cite{educonvokit} surface useful feedback from transcripts,
and transformer models can detect productive talk
moves~\cite{talkmovesaaai}, yet no pipeline jointly models strategy quality
and student affect to surface actionable friction moments.
Confusion has been identified as a pivotal affective state that, when
unaddressed, impedes deep learning~\cite{dmello2012}.
Large vision-language models such as Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} now make
it feasible to synthesise video frames, transcripts, and affect signals in
a single inference pass.

We introduce a framework that operationalises \emph{pedagogical
friction}---the joint occurrence of a risky instructional strategy and
elevated student confusion---and produces a post-session
\emph{Teacher Friction Report} with timestamps and suggested instructional
moves.  Our contributions are: (i)~a working end-to-end multimodal
pipeline; (ii)~a public codebase at
\texttt{github.com/jw310-wjr/comp646-friction-detector}; and
(iii)~preliminary results on two public TIMSS lessons~\cite{timss1999video}.

\section{Methodology}

Figure~\ref{fig:model} shows a schematic overview.

\textbf{Vision stream.}
Frames are sampled at $r{=}2$\,Hz.
DeepFace~\cite{deepface} estimates per-frame emotion probabilities, which
are mapped to a scalar confusion proxy $c_t\!\in\![0,1]$ via a fixed
weight table: neutral/happy\,$\to\!0$; fear/sad\,$\to\!0.85$--$0.9$;
disgust/angry\,$\to\!0.45$--$0.5$.
A trailing 30-second mean yields the confusion timeline $C(t)$.

\textbf{Language stream.}
faster-Whisper~\cite{whisper} transcribes audio with VAD filtering.
A \emph{heuristic strategy annotator} flags low-quality moves
(\eg \textit{``the formula is\ldots''}) and high-pressure moves
(\eg \textit{``that's wrong''})---a drop-in for future
Edu-ConvoKit / Tutor CoPilot~\cite{educonvokit,tutorcopilot} classifiers.

\textbf{Temporal alignment and filtering.}
Sessions are partitioned into $G{=}30$-second bins.
Per-bin mean confusion $\bar{c}_k$ and strategy quality label
$q_k\!\in\!\{\text{high},\text{low},\text{unknown}\}$ are computed.
A bin is flagged as a \emph{friction candidate} when
\begin{equation}
  \bar{c}_k > \mu_C + z\,\sigma_C
  \quad\text{and}\quad q_k \neq \text{high},
  \label{eq:filter}
\end{equation}
where $\mu_C$, $\sigma_C$ are the session-level mean and s.d.\ of confusion
scores, and $z$ is a sensitivity parameter.
Setting \texttt{flag\_unknown\_strategy=True} treats
$q_k{=}\text{unknown}$ as potentially risky when heuristics lack coverage.

\textbf{VLM fusion (optional).}
For each candidate, Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} receives 1--4 frames,
a confusion summary, strategy label, and transcript excerpt, returning
structured JSON: \texttt{friction}\,(bool), \texttt{rationale}\,(str),
\texttt{alternative\_strategy}\,(str).

\section{Experiments and Results}

\textbf{Setup.}
Two public TIMSS lessons~\cite{timss1999video,timssvideo}:
\emph{AU1 Exterior Angles} (geometry, Australian Grade~8) and
\emph{TIMSS Math RUB} (Russian Grade~8).
CPU path: Python~3.9, OpenCV, DeepFace (TF~2.x), faster-Whisper
(\texttt{base}, int8).
Qwen (\texttt{Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct}) runs on GPU (pending).
Key hyperparameters: $r{=}2$\,Hz, $G{=}30$\,s, $z\!\in\!\{1.0,\,0.3\}$.

\textbf{Results.}
Table~\ref{table:results} summarises all conditions.
With $z{=}1.0$, the AU1 teacher's question-based discourse moves
(\textit{``What did you learn?''}, \textit{``Do you agree?''}) fall outside
our keyword vocabulary, assigning $q_k{=}\text{high}$ to every bin and
yielding \textbf{zero candidates}.
Lowering to $z{=}0.3$ and enabling \texttt{flag\_unknown\_strategy}
produces \textbf{3 candidates} at 150--240\,s, where per-bin confusion
peaks at 0.55--0.70 (Figure~\ref{fig:timeline}).
On Math RUB, Whisper \texttt{base} hallucinated a single repeated phrase
under low audio SNR~\cite{whisper}, causing all bins to receive
$q_k{=}\text{high}$ and leaving zero candidates; mitigation via a larger
model is planned.
Qwen2.5-VL fusion is fully implemented in
\texttt{src/fusion/qwen\_multimodal.py} but awaits GPU access.
Human annotation for Precision/Recall/F1 is planned as the next milestone.

\section{Conclusion}

We presented an end-to-end multimodal pedagogical friction detector,
resolved a zero-candidate bug via threshold calibration and
\texttt{flag\_unknown\_strategy}, and produced 3 preliminary candidate
detections on the AU1 TIMSS clip.
Next steps: (1)~run Qwen2.5-VL on AU1 candidates on the Rice GPU cluster;
(2)~integrate Edu-ConvoKit / Tutor CoPilot classifiers to replace heuristics;
(3)~collect dual human annotations and compute Precision/Recall/F1.
Automated facial affect inference may misread culture- or
disability-related cues; all pipeline outputs are advisory and require
human review.
