%%%%%%%%% ABSTRACT
\begin{abstract}
We study automatic detection of \emph{pedagogical friction}: moments where a low-quality instructional strategy co-occurs with visible student confusion in classroom or tutoring video. Our system ingests synchronized video and audio, builds a confusion timeline from per-frame facial affect (DeepFace), transcribes speech with Whisper, and labels teacher dialogue with heuristics that stand in for Edu-ConvoKit and Tutor CoPilot classifiers. Vision and language streams align on a 30-second grid; a statistical pre-filter proposes candidate windows for optional multimodal reasoning with Qwen2.5-VL (substituting for GPT-4o in our design). The output is a post-session \emph{Teacher Friction Report} with timestamps, strategy context, and suggested moves. We released an open implementation~\footnote{\url{https://github.com/jw310-wjr/comp646-friction-detector}} and ran CPU smoke tests on public TIMSS-style lessons; full VLM fusion and human evaluation are ongoing.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Understanding teaching quality increasingly relies on both \emph{what teachers say} and \emph{how students respond}. Prior work improves tutoring using text-based feedback (Tutor CoPilot~\cite{tutorcopilot}) and supports educational dialogue analysis (Edu-ConvoKit~\cite{educonvokit}), but many deployments overlook multimodal classroom signals such as facial affect and board work. We build a pipeline that operationalizes \emph{pedagogical friction}---alignment of strategy quality proxies with confusion---in the spirit of accountable talk research summarized in TalkMoves-style systems~\cite{talkmovesaaai}. \section{Related Work}

\textbf{Classroom discourse and feedback.} Transformer-based tools provide teachers feedback on talk moves~\cite{talkmovesaaai}. \textbf{Tutoring and chat.} Tutor CoPilot scales expert-like nudges from transcripts~\cite{tutorcopilot}. \textbf{Libraries.} Edu-ConvoKit lowers the barrier to analyzing educational conversation~\cite{educonvokit}. \textbf{Audio.} Whisper enables robust ASR for long-form speech~\cite{whisper}. \textbf{Vision--language models.} Qwen2.5-VL supports multimodal instruction following for our fusion stage~\cite{qwen25vl}. \textbf{Affect.} Off-the-shelf facial attribute stacks (e.g., DeepFace~\cite{deepface}) give a practical confusion proxy on CPU. We connect these threads into an end-to-end \emph{friction} detector rather than captioning or retrieval.

\section{Methodology}

We sample video at configurable FPS (default 2 Hz), run DeepFace emotion analysis~\cite{deepface}, map emotions to a scalar confusion proxy, and smooth with a trailing 30-second mean. In parallel, faster-Whisper~\cite{whisper} transcribes audio. Utterance-level \emph{heuristic} rules flag low-quality or high-pressure teacher moves; these are placeholders for Edu-ConvoKit + Tutor CoPilot~\cite{educonvokit,tutorcopilot} checkpoints. We aggregate both modalities into 30-second bins, mark bins whose confusion exceeds $\mu + z\sigma$ \emph{and} that exhibit risky strategy summaries, and pass representative frames plus transcript excerpts to Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} for JSON-formatted friction judgments and alternative strategies. Figure~\ref{fig:model} sketches the architecture; Table~\ref{table:results} lists planned metrics.

\section{Experimental Settings}

\section{Analysis and Discussion}

\subsection{Engineering milestones}
We completed frame extraction, affect scoring, ASR, temporal alignment, heuristic filtering, and reporting. Short clips may yield zero candidates when thresholds are conservative; we will document sensitivity to $z$ and sampling rate.

\subsection{Fusion and compute}
Qwen fusion is implemented but primarily validated under \texttt{--skip-fusion} on laptops with Python~3.9; GPU runs are scheduled for fusion ablations.

\subsection{Ethical posture preview}
Public TIMSS materials impose usage terms; affect inference can misread identity- or culture-dependent cues. Downstream use should keep teachers in the loop (see Sec.~\ref{sec:ethics}).

\section{Conclusion}

We presented a multimodal pedagogical friction detector with public code, TIMSS-aligned resources, and a CVPR-formatted progress report. Next steps fuse Qwen outputs at scale, replace heuristics with trained classifiers, and evaluate against dual human annotations.

\section{Ethical and Societal Considerations}
\label{sec:ethics}
Classroom video may involve minors; we restrict experiments to publicly released lessons and follow distributor terms~\cite{timssvideo}. Automated affect can bias outcomes; reports are advisory, not high-stakes. We recommend human review, transparent thresholds, and future work on fairness across student populations.
