%%%%%%%%% ABSTRACT
\begin{abstract}
Effective teaching depends on noticing student confusion and adjusting
instruction.  We present a system that detects \emph{pedagogical friction}:
moments where a low-quality instructional strategy coincides with visible
student confusion.  Given a classroom or tutoring recording, our pipeline
constructs a confusion timeline from per-frame facial affect (DeepFace), a
strategy timeline from automatically transcribed speech (Whisper) annotated
by dialogue-level heuristics, temporally aligns both signals on a 30-second
grid, and flags candidate windows via a statistical $z$-score filter.
Flagged windows are optionally passed to Qwen2.5-VL for multimodal
friction confirmation and alternative-strategy generation.  The output is a
post-session \emph{Teacher Friction Report} with timestamps, strategy
context, and suggested instructional moves.  We release an open codebase
and evaluate on public TIMSS video lessons.
CPU smoke tests on the AU1 \emph{Exterior Angles} clip (first 5\,min)
yield \textbf{3 friction candidates} after threshold calibration;
Qwen2.5-VL fusion and human-label evaluation are ongoing.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

High-quality teaching requires moment-by-moment diagnosis of student
understanding and rapid strategy adjustment~\cite{sherinvanes2009}.  Text-only tools such as
Tutor CoPilot~\cite{tutorcopilot} and Edu-ConvoKit~\cite{educonvokit}
surface useful feedback from dialogue transcripts, but overlook
complementary multimodal signals available in video: facial affect, gaze,
and board work.  We introduce a framework that operationalises
\emph{pedagogical friction}---the joint occurrence of a risky
instructional strategy and elevated student confusion---and surfaces
candidate intervals with actionable feedback.

We build on three observations: (1) DeepFace-based emotion estimates
provide a practical confusion proxy without specialised hardware;
(2) dialogue classifiers (Edu-ConvoKit, Tutor CoPilot) can identify
low-quality moves from transcripts; (3) large vision-language models such
as Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} can synthesise both modalities and produce
structured suggestions grounded in accountable-talk frameworks
\cite{talkmovesaaai}.

Our contributions are: a working end-to-end pipeline, a public
repository\footnote{\url{https://github.com/jw310-wjr/comp646-friction-detector}},
TIMSS-aligned transcript resources, and preliminary results on two public
TIMSS lesson clips (Figure~\ref{fig:leadfigure}).

\section{Related Work}

\textbf{Teacher noticing.}
Professional vision---identifying and interpreting significant events
during instruction---is central to adaptive teaching~\cite{sherinvanes2009}.
Our system provides automated multimodal triggers to support teachers'
post-session reflection on such moments.

\textbf{Dialogue-based teaching support.}
Transformer models trained on classroom transcripts detect and score talk
moves~\cite{talkmovesaaai}.  Tutor CoPilot~\cite{tutorcopilot} translates
expert strategies into real-time nudges; Edu-ConvoKit~\cite{educonvokit}
lowers the barrier to replicating such analyses.

\textbf{Multimodal reasoning.}
Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} supports image--text instruction following
with strong zero-shot generalisation; we use it in lieu of GPT-4o for
cost and reproducibility.

\textbf{Affect from video.}
Confusion has been shown to be a critical affective state during
complex learning~\cite{dmello2012}.
DeepFace~\cite{deepface} provides a practical confusion proxy without
specialised sensors, enabling CPU-friendly inference in classroom settings.

\textbf{Speech recognition.}
Whisper~\cite{whisper} provides robust, language-agnostic ASR for
long-form classroom recordings; its built-in VAD filter reduces false
segments.

\section{Methodology}

Figure~\ref{fig:model} gives a schematic overview of our pipeline.

\textbf{Vision stream.}
Frames are sampled at $r$\,Hz (default $r{=}2$; reduced for CPU tests).
DeepFace~\cite{deepface} estimates per-frame emotion probabilities mapped
to a scalar confusion proxy $c_t\!\in\![0,1]$ via a fixed weight table
(neutral/happy\,$\to$\,0; fear/sad\,$\to$\,0.85--0.9;
disgust/angry\,$\to$\,0.45--0.5).  A trailing 30-second mean yields
confusion timeline $C(t)$.

\textbf{Language stream.}
faster-Whisper~\cite{whisper} transcribes audio with VAD filtering.  A
\emph{heuristic strategy annotator} applies regular-expression patterns
for low-quality moves (\eg \textit{``the formula is\ldots''}) and
high-pressure moves (\eg \textit{``that's wrong''}).  This annotator is a
drop-in for Edu-ConvoKit and Tutor CoPilot~\cite{educonvokit,tutorcopilot}
classifiers planned for a later milestone.

\textbf{Temporal alignment and filtering.}
Sessions are partitioned into $G{=}30$-second bins.  Per-bin mean
confusion $\bar{c}_k$ and strategy quality label
$q_k\!\in\!\{\text{high},\text{low},\text{unknown}\}$ are computed.
A bin is flagged as a \emph{friction candidate} when
\begin{equation}
  \bar{c}_k > \mu_C + z\,\sigma_C
  \quad\text{and}\quad q_k \neq \text{high},
  \label{eq:filter}
\end{equation}
where $\mu_C$, $\sigma_C$ are the session mean and s.d., and $z$ is a
sensitivity parameter.  We treat \textit{unknown} quality as potentially
risky when heuristic classifiers lack coverage (\texttt{flag\_unknown\_strategy=True}).

\textbf{VLM fusion (optional).}
For each candidate window, Qwen2.5-VL~\cite{qwen25vl} receives 1--4
representative frames, the confusion summary, strategy label, and
transcript excerpt, and returns JSON:
\texttt{friction}\,(bool), \texttt{rationale}\,(str),
\texttt{alternative\_strategy}\,(str).

\textbf{Stretch goal.}
An optional OCR module (Tesseract) can append board/slide text to the VLM
context; not yet wired into the main pipeline.

\section{Experimental Settings}

\textbf{Data.}
Two public TIMSS lessons from the TIMSS 1999 Video Study~\cite{timss1999video}:
\emph{AU1 Exterior Angles} (geometry, Australian Grade~8) and
\emph{TIMSS Math RUB} (Russian Grade~8, YouTube mirror).
Transcript archives are bundled in the repo; video files are
gitignored and added locally following TIMSSVIDEO terms~\cite{timssvideo}.

\textbf{Software.}
CPU path: Python~3.9, OpenCV, DeepFace (TF~2.x), faster-Whisper
(CTranslate2), imageio-ffmpeg (\texttt{requirements-pipeline.txt}).
Qwen path: Python~$\geq$3.10, HuggingFace \texttt{transformers},
\texttt{qwen-vl-utils} (\texttt{requirements.txt}).
Smoke tests ran on a MacBook Pro (CPU only).

\textbf{Key hyperparameters.}
Frame rate $r{=}2$\,Hz; smoothing window 30\,s;
$z\!\in\!\{1.0, 0.3\}$ (ablated); grid $G{=}30$\,s.
Whisper \texttt{base} model, int8, VAD enabled.
Qwen: \texttt{Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct}, \texttt{max\_new\_tokens=512},
\texttt{device\_map=auto}.

\section{Analysis and Discussion}

\subsection{Threshold calibration and the zero-candidate problem}

Our initial configuration ($z{=}1.0$, explicit low-quality flag required)
yielded zero candidates on both clips.  In the AU1 clip the teacher
consistently uses question-based moves (\textit{``What did you learn?''},
\textit{``Do you agree?''}) that fall outside our keyword vocabulary,
causing all bins to receive $q_k{=}\text{high}$.  We addressed this by
(1) lowering sensitivity to $z{=}0.3$ and (2) adding
\texttt{flag\_unknown\_strategy=True}, which treats
$q_k{=}\text{unknown}$ as potentially risky when heuristics lack coverage.
With these settings, three candidates emerge at 150--240\,s where mean
confusion peaks at 0.55--0.70 (Table~\ref{table:results},
Figure~\ref{fig:timeline}).

\subsection{Whisper hallucination on Math RUB}

Whisper (\texttt{base}, CPU) produced a single repeated hallucinated
phrase for the Math RUB clip, a known failure mode under low audio
SNR~\cite{whisper}.  All bins consequently receive $q_k{=}\text{high}$,
leaving zero candidates.  Mitigations include larger model sizes
(\texttt{small}, \texttt{medium}), audio pre-processing, and explicit
hallucination detection.

\subsection{Fusion and compute}

Qwen2.5-VL is fully implemented in
\texttt{src/fusion/qwen\_multimodal.py} but has not yet been exercised on
the candidate windows due to Python~3.9 and CPU constraints.
GPU fusion is scheduled on the Rice computing cluster.

\subsection{Limitations}

Heuristic strategy tags are not substitutes for trained classifiers.
DeepFace confusion proxies may misread culture- or disability-related
cues.  Ground-truth friction labels are yet to be collected; precision and
recall in Table~\ref{table:results} are pending.

\section{Conclusion}

We presented an end-to-end multimodal pedagogical friction detector,
identified and resolved a zero-candidate bug via threshold calibration and
an \texttt{flag\_unknown\_strategy} option, and produced preliminary
candidate detections on the AU1 TIMSS clip.  Next steps: (1) run
Qwen2.5-VL fusion on AU1 candidates; (2) expand heuristic vocabulary and
integrate Edu-ConvoKit / Tutor CoPilot classifiers; (3) collect dual human
annotations for ground truth; (4) compute Precision/Recall/F1 and populate
Table~\ref{table:results}; (5) investigate Whisper hallucinations on
non-English low-SNR clips.

\section{Ethical and Societal Considerations}
\label{sec:ethics}

Classroom video may involve minors and private interactions.  We restrict
all experiments to publicly released TIMSS public-use lessons and follow
TIMSSVIDEO terms~\cite{timssvideo}.  Automated facial affect inference can
misread identity-, culture-, or disability-correlated cues; our reports are
advisory for teacher reflection, not high-stakes decisions.  We recommend
human review of all flagged windows and transparent disclaimers in any
deployed tool.  Future work should evaluate fairness across student
demographics and non-Western classroom norms.
